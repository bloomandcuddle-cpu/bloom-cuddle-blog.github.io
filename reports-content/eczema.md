Before diving into products, it helps to understand what you are treating. Is it simple dry skin, or is it atopic dermatitis (eczema)?

Dry Skin (Xerosis):

Usually caused by environmental factors (like dry air or soap), dry skin feels rough and flaky. It is often resolved quickly with a basic, fragrance-free moisturizer.

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis):

This is a chronic, underlying immune condition. Eczema patches are typically red, inflamed, intensely itchy, and may weep or crust over when severe. They often appear in common 'creases' like the elbows and behind the knees. Eczema requires specialized ingredients (like colloidal oatmeal) and heavy barrier creams to manage.

If the rash is persistently red and itchy, always consult your baby's doctor for a clear diagnosis. (Source: American Academy of Dermatology Association)
